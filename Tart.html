Chocolate Tart

Classic Elegance: A Timeless Chocolate Tart Recipe

STEPS

  1. Make the crust: In a food processor, combine the flour and salt. Pulse a few times to combine. Add the butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Slowly add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, until the dough just comes together. Press the dough into an even layer on the bottom and sides of a 9-inch tart pan. Freeze for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Blind bake the crust: Line the frozen crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove the parchment paper and pie weights and bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until the crust is lightly golden brown.
  4. Make the filling: In a medium saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until simmering. Remove from the heat and add the chocolate. Let sit for 5 minutes, then stir until smooth.
  5. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and sugar until light and fluffy. Slowly whisk in the chocolate mixture, then pour into the prebaked crust.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is just set. Let cool completely before slicing and serving.

TIPS

  • For a richer flavor, use dark chocolate instead of semisweet.
  • You can also add a pinch of espresso powder to the filling for a coffee-infused flavor.
  • Top your tart with fresh whipped cream, berries, or chocolate shavings for a beautiful and delicious presentation.
